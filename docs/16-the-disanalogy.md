# Value Between Substance and Information — the Disanalogy

> A locating doc, not a results doc. It states *where* value sits relative to the two quantities physics and
> Shannon already formalized, and *why* the same method that produced this theory cannot, by construction,
> finish it. Three moves: (1) the structure the theory imported from physics and information is the **Jaynes
> skeleton** — a potential, its Legendre dual, a conjugate (extensive, intensive) pair, and a monotone second
> law; (2) the sense in which value is *between* the two is the **Landauer interface** — substance is its
> currency, information is its structure, price is the exchange rate; (3) the one feature value has that
> *neither* substance nor information has — its reference frame is supplied by the **agent**, not the world —
> is where the subject matter of value actually lives. This doc reframes and locates. It announces no new
> theorems, and it is explicit about which of its "insights" are already in the literature. Byline: Cheng Qian.

## 0. The method, and its built-in ceiling

The theory in docs [`00`](00-thesis-seed.md)–[`05`](05-dynamics.md) was built by one move, copied from Shannon:
find the structure that the physics of *substance* and the Shannon theory of *information* genuinely share,
and transport it to value. That move has a ceiling that is worth stating at the outset, because it explains the
honest verdict the priority program reached ([`ROADMAP.md`](ROADMAP.md), "Priority-check program — final
tally"):

> Anything obtained purely by transporting a shared structure is, by construction, **already true in both
> source domains.** So the transported results are exactly the ones a reviewer can re-absorb into the source
> domain — which is why the single-agent core reads as *generalized Kelly* and the alignment-stability result
> reads as *classical control*. The analogy can only hand back what physics and information already share.

This doc takes that ceiling seriously and turns it into a map: §1 names the shared structure that was imported
(and verifies each identity against the source docs); §2 names the precise sense of "between"; §3 locates the
one region the analogy *cannot* reach — and is careful to concede that the results already found there are
anticipated too, leaving only one genuinely-open candidate, flagged with the pre-check it still owes.

## 1. The imported invariant — the Jaynes skeleton

What physics and information *share* is not "entropy the number." Jaynes (1957) showed the deeper object:
statistical mechanics and inference are the same mathematics — a **convex potential, its Legendre dual, a
conjugate (extensive, intensive) coordinate pair, and a second law that says the potential moves one way.**
Thermodynamics instantiates it as `S(U)` ↔ free energy `F(T)` with conjugate pairs `(U, T)`, `(N, μ)`;
information theory instantiates it as the negative-entropy potential `F(p)=Σ pᵢ ln pᵢ` ↔ the log-partition /
cumulant-generating function, with the Bregman divergence of `F` being the KL divergence (Amari; Banerjee et
al. 2005). The two are not analogous — they are the same skeleton.

The theory imports that skeleton cleanly. Each identity below is in the source docs, not new here:

| Jaynes skeleton | Value instance | Source |
|---|---|---|
| a potential of definite convexity | `V(e) = Σᵢ kᵢ ln eᵢ` — concave (diminishing returns) | [`01`](01-core-formalization.md) §3 |
| its Legendre dual → an **intensive conjugate** | `λ = ∂V*/∂E = K/E`, with `V*(E) = K ln E − K·H(k̂)` | [`01`](01-core-formalization.md) §4, [`03`](03-cross-frame-value.md) §2 |
| the conjugate (extensive, intensive) pair | `(E, λ)` — resource and its marginal value | [`03`](03-cross-frame-value.md) §2 |
| the **Bregman divergence** of the potential | `D(q‖p)` — KL is the Bregman divergence of `Σ p ln p` | [`02`](02-coding-theorem-of-value.md) §4 |
| the **second law** (the potential moves monotonically) | `G = D(q‖r) − D(q‖p)` — realized = available − dissipated | [`02`](02-coding-theorem-of-value.md) §4 |

Three of these deserve a verifying word, because the doc asserts them:

- **The intensive conjugate is exact, not loose.** `V*(E) = K ln E − K·H(k̂)` ([`01`](01-core-formalization.md)
  §4), so `λ = ∂V*/∂E = K/E` ([`03`](03-cross-frame-value.md) §2). `λ` is the marginal value of resource — the
  *shadow price* of free energy — and it plays exactly the role of a chemical potential / inverse temperature:
  an intensive variable conjugate to the extensive resource `E`. That `λ` *equalizes* across agents at
  equilibrium ([`03`](03-cross-frame-value.md) §2) is the thermodynamic equilibration of an intensive variable,
  the same shape as temperatures equalizing in thermal contact.
- **The Bregman / second-law reading is the source docs', conceded to its origin.** `D(q‖p)` is the Bregman
  divergence generated by the convex negative-entropy `F(p)=Σ pᵢ ln pᵢ`; the value second law
  `G = D(q‖r) − D(q‖p)` is a difference of two such divergences. The identity "model inefficiency = dissipation"
  this rests on is **not ours**: it is the thermodynamics of prediction (Still, Sivak, Bell & Crooks 2012) and
  the relative-entropy-as-free-energy identity (H. Qian 2001), credited as such in
  [`related-work.md`](related-work.md).
- **Convexity, stated honestly.** The value potential `V` is *concave* in `e` (diminishing returns); the
  Bregman generator `F(p)` is *convex*. Thermodynamic potentials come in both flavors, Legendre-paired. The
  claim is only that value carries the *same* potential/dual/divergence machinery — not that any one function
  is convex where the source's is.

**Net:** the spine of the theory is the Jaynes skeleton. The recurrence of `H`, `D`, `I`, and the Fisher–Rao
metric across docs 01–04 is not decorative — it is what you get when you transport the one structure physics
and information actually share. And, per §0, it is also why those results do not by themselves escape their
source domains.

## 2. The "between" — value at the Landauer interface

Substance and information are not on a line with value in the middle by metaphor; there is a precise interface
where they touch, and value lives on it. Landauer (1961) and Bennett (1982) located the interface: information
becomes physical exactly when it must be paid for — erasing one bit costs `kT ln 2` of free energy. The
currency of that payment is free energy / exergy. Reading the theory's three pieces against that interface:

- **Currency = substance.** Value is denominated in the agent's irreplaceable resource — free energy / exergy
  ([`00`](00-thesis-seed.md) §3). This is the costly, physical, conserved-substrate side.
- **Structure = information.** Value is organized entirely by the information calculus — `H` as the focus
  penalty ([`01`](01-core-formalization.md) §4), `D` as available-minus-dissipated ([`02`](02-coding-theorem-of-value.md)
  §4), `I` as the ceiling ([`02`](02-coding-theorem-of-value.md) §3), Fisher–Rao as the cross-frame invariant
  ([`03`](03-cross-frame-value.md) §4).
- **Exchange rate = price.** The conversion factor between the two is the agent's shadow price `λ`
  ([`03`](03-cross-frame-value.md) §2) — how many joules of substrate a unit of informational edge is worth in
  this frame.

On this reading the headline theorem is an exchange-rate law. `ΔG ≤ I(X;Y)` ([`02`](02-coding-theorem-of-value.md)
§3) equates a substance-side increment (growth of the resource budget) to an information-side quantity (nats of
mutual information between world and perception). Value is the conversion factor between *nats of edge* and
*joules of substrate*, set by the goal. That is what "value is between substance and information" means once it
is made precise: value is the priced coupling at the interface where information is cashed out against physical
work.

**Priority — this reading is not fresh, and the doc must not present it as such.** Locating value/dissipation
at the information–substance interface is the **thermodynamics-of-information lineage**: Landauer (1961) and
Bennett (1982) for the interface itself; Jaynes (1957) for the shared skeleton; **Still, Sivak, Bell & Crooks
(2012)**, "thermodynamics of prediction," for the sharp modern statement that nonpredictive retained
information *equals* thermodynamic dissipation; and Friston's free-energy principle (2010) for agents as
free-energy minimizers astride the same interface. We claim only the **value-theoretic packaging** — the
identification of the interface's exchange rate with the agent's price `λ`, and the reading of `ΔG ≤ I(X;Y)` as
the exchange-rate theorem — not the interface idea, which is theirs.

## 3. The disanalogy — value's reference is agent-supplied

The analogy's ceiling (§0) forces the question: what does value have that *neither* substance nor information
has? There is exactly one structural feature, and it is the organizing idea of this doc.

**Substance and information both receive their reference from the world.** A physical system's reference is a
Hamiltonian / a spacetime frame the world supplies; an information source's reference is a distribution `r` —
the prior, the baseline, the channel statistics — that the world (or a model matched to it) supplies. In both
source domains the "from what" is given by what is.

**Value's reference is not.** Decompose the theory's three flows by where their target comes from
([`05`](05-dynamics.md) §0):

| flow | target | supplied by |
|---|---|---|
| beliefs `p` | the true law `q` | the **world** (reality) |
| prices `π` | market-clearing | the **world** (the resource constraint) |
| goals `k` | — | **none the world provides** |

Beliefs and prices flow toward targets the world hands them; they are *learnable*. Goals have no
world-given target — reality contains no fact about what *ought* to be valued — so they are *controlled* or
*selected*, never learned. Along the ladder **substance → information → value**, the reference frame migrates
*out of the world and into the agent*. The goal `k̂` is the one coordinate the world does not pin. That is the
disanalogy, and it is the precise sense in which value is a genuinely third quantity rather than a
re-skinning of the first two: the part of value with no analog in either source is exactly the
agent-supplied reference, and everything distinctively *value* (alignment, the sign of multi-agent
interaction, goal drift) is a consequence of it.

This is where the subject matter of value lives. It is **not**, however, a stack of new theorems — and the doc
is bound by the priority program to say why:

- **The is/ought asymmetry's value-side is not ours.** That goals cannot be inferred from behavior — an
  "ought" cannot be read off an "is" — is **Armstrong & Mindermann (2018)**, who prove a No-Free-Lunch result
  that a policy does not uniquely decompose into a planner and a reward, and *explicitly* call it an instance
  of Hume's gap. We concede the value-side to them. Our addition is only presentational: the *two-sided*
  framing that pairs the target-less goal-update with the truth-targeted belief-update and reads the pair as a
  structural asymmetry between an agent's two update dynamics ([`05`](05-dynamics.md) §0).
- **The alignment-stability result is elementary classical control.** Holding the fleet's effective goal at
  `k*` against selection drift ([`05`](05-dynamics.md) §4, [`07`](07-alignment-stability.md)) gives a residual
  `‖Vg‖/γ` and a criterion `γ > λ_max`. These are textbook: `γ > λ_max` is high-gain stabilization of an
  unstable mode, and `‖Vg‖/γ` is the steady-state **velocity error** `1/Kᵥ` of a Type-1 system tracking a
  *ramp* — and a drifting goal is a ramp reference (Ogata; Åström & Murray). We do not claim the control
  mathematics; the contribution is the *mapping* (goal-drift-under-selection as ramp-tracking), held with
  corresponding modesty.
- **The single-agent core is generalized Kelly** (Kelly 1956), conceded throughout
  ([`related-work.md`](related-work.md)).

So the disanalogy is the right place to *look*, and the results already found there are anticipated. Its value
to the theory is as the **organizing claim** — value is the quantity whose reference frame is agent-supplied,
and that single fact is what neither substance nor information can host — not as new mathematics.

## 4. The one genuinely-open candidate (and the pre-check it owes)

If a new law exists in the disanalogy region, it concerns the *unpinned* coordinate. The single candidate that
is both under-claimed and plausibly testable is:

> **The thermodynamic cost of maintaining a goal/frame against a drifting world.** Beliefs that track a drifting
> world owe a perpetual dissipation floor `D_min ∝ ‖q̇‖·σ` (the Dynamical Second Law,
> [`05`](05-dynamics.md) §1). The conjecture is that holding a goal fixed (or holding the fleet's effective goal
> at `k*` against selection) against a changing world likewise owes an *irreducible exergy cost* — a lower
> bound on the work a controller must spend forever, set by the drift speed.

This is the most promising candidate because it concerns the coordinate the world does not pin, and it is
checkable in a controlled multi-agent setup — the instrument gate the convergence finding
([`ROADMAP.md`](ROADMAP.md)) says everything else waits on.

**But it has neighbors, and owes a priority pre-check before any novelty claim.** Two specific reasons for
caution:

1. **The belief-side floor is already Still 2012's turf.** `D_min ∝ ‖q̇‖·σ` as stated in
   [`05`](05-dynamics.md) §1 is a *belief*-tracking floor — beliefs have a world-given target `q`, so this is
   the cost of tracking a *changing* environment, which is exactly the regime of Still et al. (2012)
   (predictive-information dissipation). The seed therefore lives on the side of value that is **not**
   distinctive. The conjecture's only escape route is the *goal* side, where there is no target — but that is
   precisely the part not yet derived.
2. **The control residual is already classical control.** The tracking residual `‖Vg‖/γ` is the conceded
   velocity error of §3. So the genuinely-new content, if any, cannot be the residual *magnitude* — it must be
   the *exergy / dissipation lower bound* of running the controller against drift, a thermodynamic statement,
   not a tracking-error one. Adaptive control already has drift-residual bounds that scale with drift speed
   (σ-modification / e-modification; Ioannou & Sun; Narendra & Annaswamy), and a drifting goal versus a
   controller is structurally a drifting-parameter tracking problem.

**Pre-check run — verdict: ANTICIPATED (the candidate does not survive; thread closed).** A focused priority
pass (the same adversarial method as the four prior passes, 2026-06-06) tried hard to break the goal-side
candidate against the housekeeping-heat / NESS-maintenance lineage, Still 2012, adaptive-control drift residuals,
self-evidencing (FEP), and feedback thermodynamics (Sagawa–Ueda). It does not survive:

- **The floor already exists, drift-independent part included.** The irreducible dissipation required to *hold* a
  system away from equilibrium — even with no drift — is **housekeeping heat**: "the dissipated heat necessary to
  maintain the violation of detailed balance in a nonequilibrium steady state" (Speck & Seifert 2005,
  *J. Phys. A* 38, L581; integral fluctuation theorem `⟨e^{−βQ_hk}⟩ = 1`). The *drift-dependent* part — the
  cost that scales with how fast the world's parameters change — is **excess heat** and its Clausius-like
  inequality for transitions between steady states (Hatano & Sasa 2001, *PRL* 86, 3463). Together these *are* "an
  irreducible exergy floor on maintaining a reference against a drifting world, set by the drift speed."
- **The decisive discriminator fails.** The candidate was meant to survive only if a floor exists for a
  *world-unpinned, agent-chosen* reference, distinct from maintaining a *given* one. But both primary results are
  explicit that the framework is **setpoint-origin-agnostic**: housekeeping heat is valid "for arbitrary driven
  transitions between steady states... regardless of how the nonequilibrium condition is established" (Speck &
  Seifert), and the steady-state-thermodynamic framework "applies to any steady state defined by control
  parameters, regardless of the physical origin" (Hatano & Sasa). Dissipation depends on the steady-state
  currents and the parameter-change protocol — **not on the semantic origin of the setpoint.** An agent-chosen
  reference and a world-given one cost the same to hold; the "chosen vs given" distinction is invisible to the
  thermodynamic accounting, so it opens no gap. (Self-evidencing / Markov-blanket maintenance, Friston, is a
  special case — maintaining the *self* as the reference — and is priced by the same housekeeping cost;
  Sagawa–Ueda feedback bounds price maintaining a state via measurement, also setpoint-agnostic.)
- **The one genuinely goal-specific feature is a corollary, not a law.** What *is* specific to the unpinned
  coordinate is that the predictive-information discount available for a world-given target — better prediction
  lowers the tracking floor (Still, Sivak, Bell & Crooks 2012, *PRL* 109, 120604) — is **unavailable** when there
  is no world target to predict. But that is not a new dissipation floor; it is the statement that the belief-side
  reduction tool does not apply on the goal side, which follows directly from the is/ought asymmetry already
  conceded (goals have no world-given target, [`05`](05-dynamics.md) §0; value-side conceded to Armstrong &
  Mindermann 2018). The floor itself is unchanged housekeeping + excess heat.

**Net:** the disanalogy yields no genuinely-new *law*. It remains the right place to look — it correctly locates
where the distinctively-value subject matter lives (§3) — but the one forward candidate that looked open is
anticipated by steady-state thermodynamics. The honest outcome is to say so and close the thread. The
distinctively-value contribution stays where the priority program already put it: the *synthesis*, the fleet
ceiling, the value/price frame split, and the governance mapping — held with the modesty those concessions
require. (Recorded in [`ROADMAP.md`](ROADMAP.md).)

## 5. What this doc did and did not do

- **Did:** named the imported structure (the Jaynes skeleton, §1, identities verified against 01–03); made
  "value is between substance and information" precise as the Landauer interface (§2, credited to the
  thermodynamics-of-information lineage); and located the one feature value has that neither source has — the
  agent-supplied reference — as the organizing idea (§3).
- **Did not:** announce a new theorem. The is/ought value-side is Armstrong & Mindermann (2018); the
  alignment-stability law is classical control; the single-agent core is generalized Kelly; the
  interface reading is Still 2012 / Landauer / FEP. The disanalogy is where the subject matter *lives*, and its
  one forward candidate (§4) was pre-checked and found **anticipated** by steady-state thermodynamics
  (housekeeping + excess heat; Speck & Seifert 2005; Hatano & Sasa 2001) — the thread is closed, no new law
  claimed.

This is a reframing-and-locating doc, in the spirit the whole program is held to: the credibility of the *good*
result is worth more than the appearance of a *great* one.
